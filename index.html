Curatorial Statement

The exhibit gathers works that move from scenes of violence and oppression to images of freedom, patriotism, unity, and national effort.

Read together, these works present recurring themes of abuse of power, suffering, resistance, patriotism, cultural pride, and the collective work of building a nation.

Reflection

Shared ideas across the collection

Together, the artworks trace themes of colonial conflict, oppression, resistance, patriotism, unity, and national pride. They present Philippine history as a story shaped by struggle, sacrifice, and collective effort.

01

Conflict

History shown through struggle

Several works focus on violence, oppression, and instability, showing how conflict becomes part of historical memory and public meaning.

02

Resistance

Freedom as a collective pursuit

The collection repeatedly points to resistance, courage, and patriotism, suggesting that freedom is achieved through shared effort rather than individual action alone.

03

Nation-Building

Unity as a recurring image

The works also emphasize unity, bayanihan, and national pride, presenting the nation as something built and defended through cooperation.
